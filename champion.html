League of Legends Champions
Our favorite champions and why we main them

When League of Legends was first released back in 2009, there were 40 champions, and from there, new champions have continuously been added. With a current roster of 159 champions, it’s difficult to pick your main, a champion who you truly enjoy and love. Here we share our favorites to shed light on these champions and hopefully inspire you to pick your own as well.

Netzero / Thresh, The Chain Warden

"Screaming won't do you any good, but it's music to my ears."

Brimmed with spite and hatred, this devilish abomination fills fear into the hearts of enemy players across the map, but surprisingly, this champion is not at all capable of single handedly carrying games. But instead offers a more supporting role of saving teammates in precarious situations with his lantern, and he could always fulfill the role of being a grim reaper to those who are foolish enough to enter into the range of his deadly hook, long enough for teammates to capitalize on the moment and burst down the foe while the Devil locks him down.

Cyanide / Jinx, The Loose Cannon

"Rules are made to be broken... like buildings! Or people!"

For Jinx, the Loose Cannon isn’t just a nickname, it's an understatement. Experiencing a surge of popularity from the TV series Arcane, Jinx can prove she also excels in the actual game as one of the best late game carries you can rely on. The auto-attack reliant champion strives in the midst of chaos supplying a barrage of bullets and cannons on the enemies from afar. But because of her frailty and dependence on items, she may have a pretty hard time in the first 10 minutes of the game, but if you let her rack up Exp. points and gold, you will suffer.

Zekaida / Zed, The Master of Shadows

"I am the blade in the darkness."

Zed, arguably the most popular champion in the game, especially because he appeared in the most famous League of Legends highlight of all time, both as the victor and the vanquished. As one of the hardest champions in the game, Zed never disappoints, it is merely in the capability of the player to take advantage of his brute power to single handedly win games. As an assassin, Zed always seeks out high-priority targets who he can kill in under a second to disable the damage capability of the enemy team, while getting away unscaved. Zed takes years of practice to master, but being one of the most fun champions to play in the entire game, the benefits of proficiency are endless.

Bionic Ace 2 / Ivern, The Greenfather

"I tread lightly so as not to hurt the dandelions."

Treading away from the champions who relish in brutal murder, Ivern sheds a dim tone and welcomes all players of all skill levels to play him. Although not as popular as the ranks of those mentioned above, playing Ivern brings a breath of fresh air to those willing to give him a try. Although a champion who excels in the Jungle, Ivern carves out a niche as the only enchanter jungler in the game, graciously giving out shields and dashes to champions who have a hard time surviving in a teamfight. Ivern is far from dependent though, as his best friend Daisy, could be summoned to give out an all-out pummeling. (ok not too nice after all)

Ornn ,The Mountainsmith

"My work speaks in the roar of flame and the ringing of steel."

While Ornn might be secluded and ill-tempered on the outside, but being his teammate is a straight up blessing. What's more to ask? He can set up extremely good teamfights with his ultimate ability, as well as disabling the enemy with his unique status affliction named Brittle, he causes great joy when seen in your own team and malice if seen on the enemy. But there’s more! With Ornn, you can channel your inner blacksmith by making custom items for your team to make them stronger, giving you a permanent advantage against the enemy team. Remember that he can do all this even if he is behind. So if you want consistency, pick up Ornn and you’ll have a great time.

Shaco, The Demon Jester

"Now you see me, now you don't!"

Clowns are so many people’s nightmares, but you know what’s worse than a clown? A clown possessed by a demonic entity hell-bent on watching the world burn. Unlike other champions, Shaco does not specialize in damage or teamfights, he capitalizes on something far, far worse. FRUSTRATION. Shaco is possibly the most annoying champion in the game with a bevy of tricks up his sleeve, which include invisibility, scary boxes and a clone just for good measure. Be warned though, Shaco is so annoying don’t be surprised if people flame you for picking him, even your teammates will look at you in disgust, but who are they to stop you?

Braum, The Heart of the Freljord

"The darker the night the brighter the stars."

If there is one champion who is defined by protecting their friends and throwing themselves into the brunt of dangers it has got to be Braum. He has a huge shield for Christ’s sake. While most other champions tend to be selfish and reclusive, Braum stands as a pillar of hope to those who have given up their quest to win the game. If Braum picks a specific member of his team to protect, rest assured, they will not die on his watch. Nothing can go through his unbreakable will and shield and if it gets tough, he won’t hesitate to make the ultimate sacrifice to win the game, and maybe even your heart : ) If you need help, just call Braum.

Pantheon, The Unbreakable Spear

"The heavens do not fear me because I am a god. They fear me because I am a man!"

The peerless warrior known as Pantheon is a nigh-unstoppable paragon of battle. Once an unwilling host to the Aspect of War, Atreus survived when the celestial power within him was slain, refusing to succumb to a blow that tore stars from the heavens. In time, he learned to embrace the power of his own mortality, and the stubborn resilience that goes along with it. Atreus now opposes the divine as Pantheon reborn, his unbreakable will fueling the fallen Aspect's weapons on the field of battle.

Yasuo, The Unforgiven

"Honor is in the heart not the name."

Yasuo is a name infamous for his difficulty and his contradictory popularity, and by popular i’m talking about the most used champion in the entire game, type of popular. Though far from consistent, streamers and pros like Moe, Pz Zang and Arkadata have etched their names in immortality because of their unbelievable proficiency on the Wind Samurai. Many young budding players get into the game because of the awe factor on Yasuo when seen in highlights, only to fall into misery when discovering the true hardships of using the actual champion. But just like Zed, master this champion and the rewards will be endless.

Jhin, The Virtuoso

"Each bullet is a piece of my soul. Each shot is a piece of me."

Dubbed as the greatest designed champion in the game, Jhin will never disappoint. Though released in 2016, his lore, gameplay, kit and fun factor is still unrivaled even until this day. Riot’s magnum opus is far from just smoke and mirrors. His kit is stemmed from snipers from FPS games so you better have good aim! But as a sniper you know his bullets will hurt, a lot. He has everything, traps, mobility, lockdown and brute force. Not to mention his ultimate, Curtain Call. Forcing enemies to dance in his own sick stage being sprayed with 4 bullets, with each one being stronger and faster than the last. Jhin is a delusional murderer that aims for a perfect performance, do your best to not disappoint him.
